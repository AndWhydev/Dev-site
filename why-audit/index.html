The Discovery Audit

Most founders don’t find out
until it’s too late.

You just got off a call with Andy. What he told you about the Discovery Audit is real. This is the full picture of what it covers, and what happens to founders who skip it.

Most agencies take your brief, build what you ask for and send an invoice. Nobody checks whether you legally own the code. Nobody checks whether your payment setup will get your account frozen. Nobody checks whether you are sitting on a six figure compliance exposure. That is what the Discovery Audit is for.

You could spend $50,000 building something that looks fine, works fine, and is a liability underneath. You would not know. Until something goes wrong.

Risk Breakdown

Every risk area the Discovery Audit covers.

Across the categories below, the combined regulatory and legal exposure for an unaudited app build can run into the millions. Privacy Act fines alone reach $50 million. A single data breach averages over $4 million in Australia. PCI non compliance runs $5,000 per month. IP ownership disputes have cost founders their entire codebase. Most of it is invisible until something triggers it. That is the point.

These are real, legally enforceable obligations. Most founders don’t know they exist until they are facing the consequences.

01
Category · 5 risks

Payments and Money Movement

Merchant of Record

If money flows through your platform between buyers and sellers, you may be legally classified as the merchant. You are responsible for every chargeback, fraud claim and refund. Stripe and PayPal freeze accounts of platforms that haven’t structured this correctly. We have seen founders lose access to their revenue overnight.

AFSL Licensing

Any app touching financial advice, lending, insurance, investment or superannuation may require an Australian Financial Services Licence. Building without one isn’t a grey area. It carries criminal penalties.

Show 3 more risks in this category Show less

PCI DSS Compliance

If your app touches card data in any way, non compliance fines start at $5,000 per month. Your payment processor can terminate your account without notice.

Buy Now Pay Later

Integrating BNPL or deferred payment features may trigger credit licensing requirements under the National Consumer Credit Protection Act. You cannot build first and apply later.

Foreign Transaction Obligations

Overseas users may trigger tax and regulatory obligations in their jurisdictions including EU VAT and US sales tax by state.

02
Category · 5 risks

Data and Privacy

Australian Privacy Act

Fines for serious breaches now reach $50 million, or three times the benefit obtained, or 30% of adjusted turnover, whichever is greater. If you are collecting any user data without proper architecture and a compliant privacy policy, this is your exposure.

GDPR

If any EU resident can access your app, even if you are based in Australia and not targeting Europe, you may be subject to GDPR. Fines reach €20 million or 4% of global annual turnover.

Show 3 more risks in this category Show less

Health Data

Apps collecting health information are subject to the Health Records Act and potentially HIPAA for US users. Breaches carry mandatory notification obligations and significantly higher penalties.

Children’s Data

COPPA and equivalent Australian obligations apply to any platform accessible by users under 13. Non compliance is grounds for immediate app store removal.

Tracking and Cookie Consent

Your analytics, ad pixels and retargeting tools may be collecting and transmitting user data without legally required consent flows. This is increasingly enforced.

03
Category · 5 risks

Cybersecurity

API Security

Exposed or unauthenticated API endpoints are the number one attack vector in modern applications. Most early stage products have zero API security strategy. Your data, your users’ data and your business logic are all accessible to anyone who knows where to look.

Cloud Misconfiguration

Publicly exposed S3 buckets, unsecured Firebase databases, unprotected environment variables and missing access controls are how most startups get breached. It looks fine from your dashboard. It is wide open from the outside.

Show 3 more risks in this category Show less

OWASP Top 10 Vulnerabilities

The ten most critical web application security risks are present in the majority of freelancer built apps. Most MVPs fail at least three of these out of the box without the developer or the founder knowing.

Dependency Vulnerabilities

Every open source library in your tech stack is a potential attack vector. If those libraries haven’t been audited, you’ve inherited someone else’s security problem.

No Penetration Testing

Launching without a penetration test is the equivalent of opening a business without ever checking whether the locks work. You are inviting the first person who tries.

04
Category · 4 risks

Intellectual Property

IP Ownership of the Codebase

In Australia, copyright defaults to the creator, not the person who paid, unless there is a written IP assignment agreement. If a freelancer or contractor built any part of your product without one in place, you may not legally own your own app.

Open Source Licence Compliance

Using GPL licensed code in a commercial product can legally require you to open source your entire codebase. Most developers drop in open source libraries without checking the licence terms. This can destroy your IP position overnight.

Show 2 more risks in this category Show less

Third Party API Terms of Service

Building a business on top of another platform without understanding their commercial use terms is a significant risk. Platforms change terms, restrict commercial use or shut down APIs with limited notice. Your business model may not survive it.

Trademark Clearance

Launching under a name without checking whether it is already registered is one of the most common and most expensive mistakes founders make. A cease and desist six months after launch with a brand and marketing spend behind you is a serious problem.

05
Category · 5 risks

Legal Structure and Liability

Limitation of Liability

If your app fails or results in a user experiencing financial loss, what is your legal exposure without a properly drafted limitation of liability clause? In many cases: unlimited.

App Store Compliance

Apple and Google have their own rules on subscriptions, in app purchases, data collection disclosures and content. They will remove your app without warning. We have seen products with tens of thousands of users pulled overnight.

Show 3 more risks in this category Show less

Terms of Service and Acceptable Use Policy

Without these, your users have no obligations and you have no protection. You cannot enforce restrictions on how your platform is used or terminate abusive users with legal standing.

Australian Consumer Law

Statutory guarantees, refund rights and misleading conduct provisions apply to digital products. They cannot be contracted out of. If your product doesn’t work as described, you have legal obligations regardless of what your terms say.

Dispute Resolution

Without a governing law clause and a clear dispute resolution mechanism, any user anywhere in the world can potentially bring a claim in their local jurisdiction.

06
Category · 2 risks

Tax and Financial

R&D Tax Incentive

Most founders building software don’t know they may be eligible for up to 43.5 cents back on every dollar of eligible development expenditure. This must be structured correctly from day one. Retroactive registration rarely captures the full benefit. AWLabs helps every client access this.

GST on Digital Services

If you have overseas customers, you may have tax obligations in their jurisdictions including EU VAT registration and US sales tax obligations that vary by state.

07
Category · 3 risks

Employment and Contractor Risk

Sham Contracting

If you are engaging contractors who work exclusively for you or under your direct control, the ATO may reclassify them as employees, triggering superannuation obligations, leave entitlements and payroll tax liabilities, potentially backdated.

IP Assignment Gaps

Every developer, contractor and consultant who contributes to your codebase needs a signed IP assignment agreement. One gap in that chain can cloud your IP ownership permanently.

Show 1 more risk in this category Show less

Equity and Co Founder Agreements

Without properly documented vesting schedules, cliff periods and exit provisions, a co founder or early team member who leaves can walk away with equity they did nothing to earn, or hold the company hostage.

08
Category · 4 risks

Regulated Industry Triggers

Health and Medical Apps

Any app that makes therapeutic claims, processes clinical data or could be used in a clinical setting may need TGA registration. This is a lengthy and expensive process. You cannot launch first and register later.

Legal Tech

The line between providing legal information and legal advice is narrow and actively enforced. Crossing it, even through an AI feature, exposes you to professional liability claims.

Show 2 more risks in this category Show less

Education and Child Safety

Platforms with minors as users carry Working With Children Check obligations, child safety policy requirements and mandatory reporting obligations that vary by state.

Property and Real Estate Features

Certain property related features may require real estate agent licensing. Penalties for unlicensed conduct are significant.

09
Category · 1 risk

Accessibility

WCAG 2.2 Compliance

Under the Disability Discrimination Act 1992, digital products must be accessible to users with disabilities. Government contracts will not proceed without WCAG compliance. Enforcement is increasing. Retrofitting accessibility into an existing product is significantly more expensive than building it in from the start.

10
Category · 3 risks

Architecture and Scalability

Wrong Tech Stack

A freelancer builds in what they know, not what is right for your scale, your use case or your long term roadmap. Rebuilding from scratch because the original architecture cannot handle growth is one of the most common and most expensive problems we see.

No Disaster Recovery Strategy

If your database goes down, what happens? Most early stage products have no backup strategy and no defined recovery time. One infrastructure event can mean permanent data loss.

Show 1 more risk in this category Show less

Vendor Lock In

Building entirely on one cloud provider or proprietary platform with no exit strategy means that provider controls your costs, your uptime and ultimately your business continuity.

11
Category · 2 risks

Insurance

Cyber Liability Insurance

The average cost of a data breach in Australia is now over $4 million. Most early stage founders have no cyber liability coverage. One breach can exceed the cost of the entire build.

Professional Indemnity

If your software causes a client financial loss, professional indemnity insurance is your protection. Without it you are personally exposed.

Real World Consequences

What happens when founders skip it.

First person because we have seen them.

Merchant of record misclassification

We had a client whose marketplace hit $2M in GMV before Stripe froze their account due to undisclosed platform transactions. They couldn’t pay their vendors for six weeks. The business nearly collapsed.

IP ownership gap

A founder paid $60,000 to a development agency under a poorly drafted contract. When the relationship broke down, the agency retained ownership of the codebase. The founder had to rebuild from scratch.

Privacy Act breach

A Sydney based startup suffered a data breach affecting 4,000 users. Without a mandatory breach response plan, they notified the OAIC 47 days late. The investigation cost more than the original build.

Cloud misconfiguration

A Firebase misconfiguration exposed the personal data of 100,000 users of an Australian fintech app. The database had been publicly readable for eight months before anyone discovered it.

App store removal

A consumer app with 30,000 active users was removed from the App Store for undisclosed data collection. It took four months and a full rebuild to get it reinstated. Four months of zero new users.

Open source licence violation

A SaaS founder built their core product on a GPL licensed library. When they went to raise their Series A, legal due diligence flagged the violation. They had to open source their product or rebuild the affected components before the round could close.

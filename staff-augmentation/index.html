How It Works

Three Steps to Your Extended Team

We've streamlined the process so you have a matched, vetted engineer ready to start within days — not weeks.

01

Brief Us

Tell us what you need: the role, the tech stack, the team context, the engagement length and when you want to start. We do not need a formal job description — a 30-minute call is enough. We ask the questions other agencies don't: what does your team culture look like? What's the biggest delivery challenge? What does failure look like in this role?

02

Match Engineers

Within 48 hours, we present two to three pre-screened candidates from our pool — not CVs from a database, but engineers we know personally, whose code we have reviewed and whose working style we can vouch for. You interview them. If none are right, we keep looking. No fee until you say yes.

03

Embed and Deliver

Your new engineer joins your Slack, your GitHub, your sprint ceremonies and your team calls. They operate as a member of your team — not a contractor who disappears after a ticket is closed. We stay in the background: monthly check-ins, performance oversight and rapid replacement if something is not working.

Quality Assurance

Our 5-Step Engineer Vetting Process

Every engineer in our pool has passed this process. We decline 85% of applicants. You should never receive a candidate who is not genuinely senior.

01

Application Screening

CV and portfolio review against senior engineer benchmarks. We filter on genuine commercial experience — not credentials. FAANG or startup, what matters is shipped production code in demanding environments.

02

Technical Assessment

A take-home assessment calibrated to the role — not a whiteboard algorithm test. Frontend engineers build a real component. Backend engineers design a system. We assess code quality, decisions made and what was asked about edge cases.

03

Live Code Review

A 60-minute live session: reviewing real production code they have written, discussing the decisions made and what they would change. This reveals far more about engineering judgment than any prepared exercise.

04

Cultural Fit Interview

A structured conversation about how they work in teams: how they give feedback, handle technical disagreements, communicate blockers and approach ambiguous requirements. Senior engineers must communicate, not just code.

05

Trial Project

For placement, a short paid trial project with our team before client placement. This validates performance under real conditions — not interview conditions — and ensures the engineer is ready to contribute from day one of your engagement.

Timezone and Communication

Australian-Based, Globally Connected

All Webbed Labs engineers operate on AEST (UTC+10) or AEDT (UTC+11). This places us in complete overlap with Australian teams and provides workable overlap for UK and US-based clients.

UK teams (GMT) have 9–11 hours overlap during summer and roughly 8 hours during Australian standard time. US West Coast teams (PST) can align start-of-day calls with Australian end-of-day for synchronous check-ins, with async handoffs covering the gap.

We work in the tools your team already uses. If you use Jira, we use Jira. If you prefer Linear, we use Linear. Communication happens in your Slack or Teams workspace — not ours.

Rates vary by role seniority, technology specialisation and engagement length. As a guide: mid-level engineers start from $700 per day, senior engineers from $950 per day, and specialist roles (AI/ML, solutions architects, engineering managers) from $1,200 per day. Longer-term commitments attract discounted rates. We provide a fixed rate card at the start of every engagement — there are no hidden charges, no markup surprises, and super and insurance are included in the stated rate.

Our benchmark is clear: five or more years of commercial experience shipping production software, at least two years in a role with genuine technical ownership (not junior tasks), able to independently design and implement features end-to-end, and capable of reviewing code and mentoring others. We decline 85% of applicants. If we recommend an engineer, it is because we would put them on our own internal projects.

Three months for part-time and full-time placements. This minimum exists because the ramp-up time — learning your codebase, team conventions, domain context — means the first four to six weeks of any placement are below peak contribution. A three-month minimum protects both sides: it gives the engineer time to add genuine value and gives you enough of a run to assess fit and performance fairly.

We have a 30-day performance review built into every new placement. If, after a reasonable period, the engineer is not working out, we will replace them at no additional placement cost. We ask for honest, documented feedback so we can brief the replacement correctly. In practice, this is rare — our vetting process and the matching conversation before placement significantly reduces mismatches.

For common roles (frontend, backend, React, Flutter) we can typically present matched candidates within 48 hours and have an engineer starting within two weeks. For specialist roles (AI/ML engineering, solutions architects, highly specific tech stacks) it may take one to two weeks to identify the right match. We will tell you honestly when we cannot move fast enough for your timeline rather than rushing a poor placement.
